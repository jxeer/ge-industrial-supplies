I thought it was better to describe the scope of work of Charter Flight Service.
1. Core Flight Services
Provide reliable aircraft and support services to move people and supplies across Africa. This includes:

    Scheduled Flights: Regular rotations between hubs.
    Ad-hoc Charters: Flexible flights for specific mission needs.
    Rapid Response: Emergency missions and medical evacuations (MEDEVAC).

2. Aircraft & Crew Requirements

    The Fleet: Supply fixed-wing planes or helicopters that are fit for "bush" flying (remote, hot, and high-altitude areas).
    Maintenance: Every aircraft must be fully certified, airworthy, and meet ICAO and UN safety standards.
    The Team: Provide highly experienced pilots and engineers accustomed to working in humanitarian settings and challenging environments.

3. Passenger & Cargo Handling

    People: Safely transport UN staff and partners. You are responsible for check-ins, manifests, and safety briefings.
    Supplies: Deliver humanitarian aid, medical equipment, and time-sensitive cargo.
    Logistics: Manage all loading, weight/balance calculations, and necessary paperwork.

4. Specialized Operations

    MEDEVAC: Keep aircraft ready for emergency medical pickups, including those requiring stretcher setups and medical equipment.
    Remote Access: Be prepared to operate from short, unpaved, or basic airstrips with minimal ground infrastructure.

5. Coordination & Safety

    Compliance: Follow all UN Aviation Safety standards and maintain a strict Safety Management System (SMS).
    Communication: Stay in constant contact with UN Operations Centers for mission tasking and flight tracking.
    Ground Support: Organize your own fueling, flight planning, and ground handling at all designated locations.

6. Readiness & Availability

    Standby: Keep aircraft and crews ready to mobilize within agreed-upon timelines.
    Flexibility: Be prepared to adjust flight paths or schedules at short notice based on changing UN operational needs.
